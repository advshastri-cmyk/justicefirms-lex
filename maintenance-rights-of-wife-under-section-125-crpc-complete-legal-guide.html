Maintenance Rights of Wife under Section 125 CrPC – Complete Legal Guide (2026)

By Justice Team • 10 min read • Published: January 2026

Section 125 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 is one of India's most powerful social justice provisions. It provides a quick, affordable, and secular remedy to prevent vagrancy and destitution by compelling a person with sufficient means to maintain his wife, children, and parents.

Who Can Claim Maintenance under Section 125?

  • Wife: Includes legally wedded wife (even if separated/divorced but not remarried)
  • Minor children: Legitimate or illegitimate
  • Adult children: Unable to maintain due to disability
  • Parents: If unable to maintain themselves

Key Conditions for Wife to Claim Maintenance

A wife can claim if:

  1. She is unable to maintain herself
  2. Husband has sufficient means but neglects/refuses

Exceptions (when claim can be denied):

  • Wife living in adultery
  • Wife refuses to live with husband without sufficient cause
  • Wife living separately by mutual consent

Procedure to File Petition

  1. File application before Judicial Magistrate (1st Class) in whose jurisdiction parties reside
  2. No court fee required (very low cost)
  3. Interim maintenance can be granted during pendency
  4. Enforcement through attachment of salary/property or even imprisonment

Quantum of Maintenance – Factors Considered

Courts consider:

  • Husband's income & lifestyle
  • Wife's needs & standard of living
  • Number of dependents
  • Inflation (2026 context – courts often award 25-40% of net income)

Important Supreme Court Judgments

  • Badshah vs Urmila (2014): Liberal interpretation; even second wife can claim in some cases
  • Bhagwan Dutt vs Kamla Devi (1975): Maintenance should be reasonable
  • Rajathi vs C. Ganesan (1999): Interim maintenance mandatory in most cases

2025-2026 Updates & Trends

With rising living costs, courts are increasingly awarding higher interim amounts (₹15,000–₹50,000+ pm depending on income). Digital filing and video hearings have made process faster in many districts.

Conclusion: Section 125 CrPC remains the fastest remedy for financial security of neglected family members. It is secular, summary, and enforceable. Consult a lawyer immediately if facing neglect – delay can weaken your case.
